Moroccan Lamb Tagine

A traditional North African dish, slow-cooked to perfection in a tagine (a cone-shaped clay pot) or a regular pot. It features tender lamb, aromatic spices, and sweet-savory flavors from ingredients like dried fruits, nuts, and honey.

Serves: 4

Prep Time: overnight | Cook Time: 2 hours

Necessary equipment:

  • Tagine
  • Frying pan
  • Large mixing bowl
  • Grater
  • Small bowl
  • Wooden spoon
  • Oven

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds lamb meat, cut into cubes
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ¾ tsp garlic powder
  • ¾ tsp ground coriander
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp ground ginger
  • ¼ tsp ground turmeric
  • ¼ tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 pinch saffron
  • 2 medium onions, grated
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp freshly grated ginger
  • 570ml/1 pint tomato juice
  • 2 x 400g tinned chopped tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp honey

Instructions:

  1. Place the cayenne, black pepper, paprika, ginger, turmeric and cinnamon into a small bowl and mix to combine. Place the lamb in a large bowl and toss together with half of the spice mix. Cover and leave overnight in the fridge.
  2. Preheat the oven to 150C/300F/Gas2.
  3. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil and 1 tbsp of argan (or vegetable) oil in a large casserole dish. Add the grated onion and the remaining spice mix and cook over a gentle heat for 10 minutes so that the onions are soft but not coloured. Add the crushed garlic for the final 3 minutes.
  4. In a separate frying pan, heat the remaining oil and brown the cubes of lamb on all sides then add the browned meat to the casserole dish. De-glaze the frying pan with ¼ pint of tomato juice and add these juices to the pan.
  5. Add the remaining tomato juice, chopped tomatoes, apricots, dates, raisins or sultanas, flaked almonds, saffron, lamb stock and honey to the casserole dish. Bring to the boil, cover with a fitted lid, place in the oven and cook for 2-2½ hours or until the meat is meltingly tender.
  6. Place the lamb in a tagine or large serving dish and sprinkle over the chopped herbs. Serve.

Chicken Couscous

A staple in Moroccan cuisine, often served for family gatherings and celebrations, made with spiced chicken, vegetables, and couscous (a light, fluffy semolina grain).

Serves: 4

Prep Time: 15 mins | Cook Time: 30 mins

Necessary equipment:

  • Large pot with lid
  • medium saucepan
  • Fork
  • Ladle
  • Cutting borad & sharp knife
  • Wooden spoon

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb (450g) chicken thighs or drumsticks
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup canned chickpeas (drained)
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup couscous
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • ¼ cup fresh cilantro or parsley, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pot. Sear the chicken until golden brown, then remove.
  2. In the same pot, sauté onion and garlic until soft.
  3. Stir in cumin, coriander, paprika, cinnamon, turmeric, salt, and pepper. Cook for 1 minute.
  4. Add carrots, zucchini, chickpeas, tomatoes, and broth. Return the chicken to the pot. Simmer for 30-40 minutes until tender.
  5. In a bowl, pour 1 cup of boiling water or broth over couscous. Cover and let it sit for 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork and stir in butter.
  6. Plate the couscous, spoon the chicken and vegetables over it, and garnish with cilantro or parsley.

Harira Soup

A traditional Moroccan, often enjoyed during Ramadan to break the fast. It's a hearty, spiced tomato-based soup made with lentils, chickpeas, and sometimes meat (usually lamb or beef). The flavors are rich and comforting, making it a beloved dish in Moroccan cuisine.

Serves: 6

Prep Time: 10 mins | Cook Time: 1 hour

Necessary equipment:

  • Large soup pot
  • Strainer
  • Ladle
  • Wooden spoon

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • 1 cup canned chickpeas (drained)
  • ½ cup lentils (red or green)
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • ½ cup cooked rice or vermicelli noodles (optional)
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • ¼ cup fresh cilantro or parsley, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pot over medium heat. Sauté onion and garlic until soft.
  2. Stir in cumin, coriander, paprika, cinnamon, turmeric, salt, and pepper. Cook for 1 minute.
  3. Add chickpeas, lentils, tomatoes, broth, and tomato paste. Stir and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 30-40 minutes, until lentils are tender.
  4. If using, stir in cooked rice or vermicelli and cook for another 5 minutes.
  5. Stir in lemon juice and garnish with cilantro or parsley.
